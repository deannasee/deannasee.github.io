TravelTHOTS

A customized trip planning app for indecisive adventurers

Overview

While working towards my Computer Science concentration at UVA, I had the opportunity to work on a UI/UX project to design an app that would help indecisive travelers discover a destination customized to their unique traits and preferences.

The app, Travel To Find Our True Selves (TravelTHOTS for short), includes a detailed questionnaire and supports an ecosystem of authentic travel reviews to personalize the trip planning experience.

GETTING STARTED

Why travel?

COVID-19 has led to significant declines in travel due to restrictions: At the onset of the pandemic, employees working in travel, tourism and outdoor recreation experienced up to 40% of earnings losses at the start of the second quarter of 2020 compared to the same time in 2019. During this period, we hypothesized that potential and seasoned travelers would experience an increased yearning for travel, along with a desire for an app to plan their future, post-COVID trips.

THE PROBLEM

No travel app currently allows users to select and plan a trip in one place, customized to their preferences.

Although various travel companion apps exist, travelers must use them in tandem in order to gain access to all the capabilities they may want. Additionally, travel safety information is provided by the US State Department, but the website exists outside of travel planner apps. Our app would centralize all the information and capabilities users might need.

How could we improve the trip planning experience, especially for indecisive travelers?

THE SOLUTION

A personalized app to help travelers select a destination based on their unique traits and preferences.

Existing travel apps such as Lonely Planet and Expedia require users to select a destination before planning their trip and often provide only generalized recommendations , while lacking personalization and authenticity. Users are expected to know exactly where they want to go from the start, despite the multitude of factors involved in making a decision. Moreover, the options these apps tout are often pricey and touristy.

In response to these problems, we developed a mobile application called TravelTHOTS (Travel To Find Our True Selves), a system that helps travelers narrow down an ideal travel location based on their unique preferences, including crowd level and COVID cases among other relevant niches. TravelTHOTS helps reduce the stress of finding travel destinations and authentic recommendations, all in a customized and efficient way.

My role

My team and I conducted research activities such as client interviews, user interviews, prototyping, and usability testing. Throughout this process, I led the research and design responsibilities, translating our feedback and low-fidelity sketches into a testable prototype.

I. Research Phase

Initial research

Our research involved a three-pronged process of reading existing literature about travel apps, using select travel apps ourselves, and engaging with active travelers about their experiences.


Gaining insights from travelers

To understand the underlying factors that influence travelers' trip-planning experiences, we conducted eight 1-1 interviews with both seasoned and inexperienced travelers. These interviews revealed five key concerns travelers have when planning travel experiences:

1

Not being able to budget accurately for the entire cost of the trip.

2

Experiencing stress due to the inability to narrow down a travel destination.

3

Feeling overwhelmed by the trip-planning process due to the logistics involved.

4

Lack of familiarity with unknown locations leads to general distrust of online reviews.

5

COVID-19 restrictions have added a layer of complication to the trip-planning process.

Modeling

We created several flow models to represent the big-picture flow of work as determined by the above concerns. In our current practice flow model, we identified our primary work roles of travelers and locals and mapped the flow of work activities between these work roles and our app. As indicated in the flow model, both locals and travelers input data into the app algorithm, which returns travel recommendations based on a consolidation of these inputs.

Focusing in on our user types

To create our personas, we took some time as a group to brainstorm, drawing common connections from our various interview notes as well as our own vision of the design, then each wrote down some basic details for some 'draft' personas.

We decided to focus on the following 5 personas, with Maddie Mitchell for our primary persona as she represented a majority of our target audience of users who are in college and eager to explore, yet overwhelmed by choice in our information-saturated digital age.

III. Evaluation

User testing

We conducted a test evaluation of this low-fidelity prototype with our client to get feedback on the product features and design. Based on her feedback, we integrated adjustments into our high-fidelity prototype, which we then tested on 7 users.

Quantitative results

Through questionnaires and surveys, we obtained quantitative subjective data about our users' experiences with the navigation and memorability of our app. We also used error counts and time measurements to get quantitative objective data about navigation and memorability. Lastly, we counted the errors per minute of usage to obtain data on the frequency of user errors.

Qualitative results

Conclusion

Through our work on TravelTHOTS, we designed an application that would appeal to adventurers looking for an easy way to start their travels. We hope that this research would not only empower first-time travelers to plan their own trip from start to finish, but also inspire other researchers to incorporate a similar user-centric approach which takes into consideration each person's specific needs.

Reflection

Challenges

One of the biggest hurdles we faced was finding appropriate users who could participate in our research. While we still gleaned important insights from our small pool of users, a larger pool would have provided more diversity in terms of feedback and potentially uncover parts of the app experience that could be made more inclusive.

Future considerations

Given more time, we hope to explore the following areas of consideration:

  1. Building a social network.While our design incorporates a user discussion forum, it can incorporate other social capabilities such as private messaging and sub-communities to enhance the user experience.
  2. System security and scalability. Given the confidential information shared in the app, ensuring that users' data is protected would be one of our top priorities. Additionally, the app would be developed to store a large amount of information as the number of users increases.

Final thoughts

As my first ever UI/UX project, I found this process incredibly rewarding and meaningful. I especially enjoyed the emphasis on people, from interviewing actual travelers and developing a prototype based on their comments, to designing an application that actually solves some issues they face.
